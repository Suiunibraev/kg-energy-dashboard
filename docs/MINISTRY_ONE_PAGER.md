# Kyrgyzstan Energy Intelligence Dashboard

## What This Tool Does

This dashboard gives Ministry staff a clear view of Kyrgyzstan's electricity situation in one place. It shows total production, total consumption, surplus or deficit, regional comparisons, and a seasonal demand forecast.

The first version uses public national data and a transparent starter regional dataset. The regional layer is ready to be replaced with official Ministry data when available.

## How To Use It

1. Open the dashboard URL.
2. Use the year range selector to choose the period you want to review.
3. Review the top summary numbers for production, consumption, hydro share, and surplus or deficit.
4. Use the national charts to compare production and consumption over time.
5. Use the regional tab to compare oblasts and identify areas with high demand or losses.
6. Open the forecast tab to see expected seasonal demand and choose a dry, normal, or wet hydropower scenario.

## What Staff Can Learn

- Whether production is keeping pace with consumption.
- Which regions have the highest electricity demand.
- Where distribution losses may deserve closer review.
- How seasonal demand may change under different water-year conditions.

## How The Ministry Can Expand It

The Ministry can connect internal data without rebuilding the dashboard. The data team can replace the starter files or connect a database/API with these fields:

- Annual national production, consumption, hydro generation, thermal generation, imports, and exports.
- Regional production, consumption, and distribution losses by year.
- Optional future upgrades: monthly demand, reservoir levels, inflows, temperature, plant availability, and planned maintenance.

With official Ministry data, the forecast can become more accurate and the regional map can become an operational monitoring tool.

## Practical Note

Forecasts should support planning discussions. They should not replace engineering judgment, dispatch procedures, or official Ministry reporting.
